Steve Jobs

Tech Visionary

Steve Jobs, founder of Apple, Inc., was a tech visionary who led a life-long quest to enrich people's lives.

A time line of Steve Jobs' life:

  • 1955 - Born in San Francisco, California.
  • 1968 - At 13 years old, Steve Jobs gets his first summer job working at Hewlett-Packard after speaking directly to Bob Hewlett.
  • 1969 - At 14, Jobs meets Steve Wozniak. Wozniak is five years older, but the two share a love for electronics.
  • 1972 - Jobs and Wozniak build and sell illegal blue boxes, allowing users to make telephone calls for free.
  • 1973 - Enrolls at Reed College, but drops out after a semester, moving to a hippie commune. Jobs later takes a job working for Atari.
  • 1976 - Wozniak and Jobs show the first board for the Apple I at the Homebrew Computer Club in Palo Alto, California. On April 1, Jobs, Wozniak, and Ron Wayne incorporate as Apple Computer.
  • 1977 - On April 17, Apple Computer debuts the Apple II at the West Coast Computer Fair.
  • 1980 - On December 12, Apple Computer goes public, making Jobs remarkably wealthy.
  • 1984 - On January 24, Macintosh is introduced at the Apple shareholder's meeting.
  • 1985 - After a coup at Apple that strips Jobs of his executive responsibilities, he, along with some other individuals from Apple, found NeXT.
  • 1986 - Jobs purchases the computer animation division of George Lucas' ILM (Industrial, Light, and Magic) and incorporates it as Pixar.
  • 1989 - As NeXT and Pixar grow, Jobs is named entrepreneur of the decade by Inc. Magazine.
  • 1991 - In May of 1991, Pixar, now solely devoted to animation with no remaining hardware production, signs to produce a feature length film with Disney. The feature film, released several years later, is called Toy Story. After the release of Toy Story, Pixar goes public, making Jobs a billionaire.
  • 1996 - In December 1996, Apple purchases the NeXT operating system and brings Jobs back onboard as an advisor. He returns to his former role as CEO in July 1997.
  • 2001 - On October 23, Apple releases the first iPod. Jobs had no idea how much it would change the company or technology.
  • 2003 - Jobs is diagnosed with pancreatic cancer, but refuses traditional medical treatments in favor of alternative therapies. He finally opts for surgery in August 2004.
  • 2007 - On January 9, Jobs introduces the item most closely associated with Apple today: the iPhone. The iPhone 3G follows in 2008.
  • 2009 - After taking a medical leave of absence, Jobs has a liver transplant.
  • 2010 - Jobs announces the release of the first tablet, the iPad, and an improved iPhone, the iPhone 4.
  • 2011 - On August 24, Jobs resigns from Apple, handing the reigns to Tim Cook.
  • 2011 - On October 5, at the age of 56, Steve Jobs dies at home, surrounded by his family.
